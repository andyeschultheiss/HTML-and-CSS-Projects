The Citroën DS

A quick introduction to an automotive masterpiece
HOME | GALLERY | VIDEO | CONTACT

  This page is devoted to the worship of the goddess, or déesse in French. It is no coincidence that the automaker Citroën chose DS as the name of this masterwork of automotive inspiration. From its bold space-age design to its breakthrough technology, the DS embodied all that was hopeful and aspirational about an era of history.

  The DS debuted in 1955 to much fanfare, although its design had been carried out over many years, and in fact the plans were hidden from the Axis during the war. And what design it had to offer; its low-slung appearance was aerodynamic and modern, every detail was thoughtfully placed. The rear turn indicators were positioned at the roofline in "trumpets" of chrome to ensure visibility due to its low, lean stature.

  But the crowning achievement, and enduring legacy of the DS, is its innovative hydropneumatic suspension. Typical automotive suspensions must exchange ride quality and suppleness for crisp handling, but lead engineer Paul Mages knew an alternative path had to exist. After years of development, Citroën debuted its solution: a system of hydraulic fluid, kept pressurized by an engine-driven pump, filled 4 spheres at the crown of each suspension attachment point, one above each wheel. Disturbances, in the form of bumps, displaced this fluid, which in turn compressed air at the top of each sphere. Such a novel system allowed for compliance to small, brief displacement in the form of road harshness, while supplying rigidity in the face of sustained compression, such as encountered in a sweeping turn at speed.
